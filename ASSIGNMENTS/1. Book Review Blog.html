Book Review: "The Things You Can See Only When You Slow Down" by Haemin Sunim

Posted by Mahi Awasthi on 29 June 2024




“When you have an uneasy feeling,
don’t grab it to inspect at it from all sides.
Leave it alone, so it can flow.
The wave of emotion will subside of its own accord
As long as you don’t feed it by letting it linger”

Introduction:

Haemin Sunim's "The Things You Can See Only When You Slow Down" is a gentle invitation to pause, reflect, and find peace amidst life's chaos. Originally published in Korean and translated into several languages, including English, this book has gained popularity for its profound yet accessible wisdom on mindfulness and living a meaningful life.

Summary:

The book is structured as a series of short reflections, each offering insights on topics ranging from love and relationships to work, stress, and spirituality. Haemin Sunim, a Buddhist monk and meditation teacher, draws upon his own experiences and Buddhist teachings to provide practical advice on how to navigate life's challenges with grace and mindfulness. Divided into thematic sections, each chapter encourages readers to slow down, observe their surroundings, and cultivate inner peace. Sunim emphasizes the importance of self-care, compassion towards oneself and others, and the value of embracing simplicity in a fast-paced world.




Review:

"The Things You Can See Only When You Slow Down" is a gem of a book that offers profound wisdom in a simple and accessible manner. What stands out immediately is the clarity and sincerity of Sunim's writing. His insights are not just philosophical ponderings but practical tools that readers can apply to their daily lives. One of the book's strengths is its brevity. Each chapter is concise yet impactful, making it easy to read and digest in short sittings. This format also encourages reflection; after reading a chapter, you may find yourself pausing to contemplate its message and how it relates to your own experiences. Sunim's approach is refreshingly non-dogmatic. While rooted in Buddhist philosophy, his advice transcends any specific religious or spiritual belief, making it accessible to readers from all backgrounds. Whether you are looking to manage stress, improve relationships, or simply find more joy in everyday life, you will find valuable insights within these pages. Moreover, the book is beautifully presented, with elegant illustrations that complement the text and enhance the overall reading experience. The combination of thoughtful prose and visual appeal creates a serene atmosphere conducive to introspection and mindfulness.

Additional Thoughts:

Haemin Sunim's "The Things You Can See Only When You Slow Down" not only offers practical wisdom but also fosters a deeper understanding of the human experience. His insights into the complexities of emotions, the importance of resilience, and the power of gratitude provide readers with tools to navigate life's inevitable challenges with greater resilience and grace. What makes this book particularly impactful is its universal appeal. Whether you are a busy professional, a student navigating academic pressures, or someone seeking solace during difficult times, Sunim's gentle guidance offers reassurance and a sense of direction. His encouragement to embrace imperfection and find beauty in the present moment resonates profoundly, urging readers to cultivate a mindset of acceptance and peace. Moreover, Sunim's anecdotes and personal reflections add a layer of authenticity that enriches the reading experience. Through his own journey as a monk and teacher, he illustrates how mindfulness can transform not only individual lives but also relationships and communities. The book's poetic language and thoughtful structure invite readers to return to its pages time and again, discovering new insights with each reading. Whether you read it cover to cover or dip into chapters as needed, "The Things You Can See Only When You Slow Down" serves as a gentle companion on the path to inner peace and self-discovery. In conclusion, this book is a testament to the power of mindfulness and compassion in creating a more meaningful and joyful existence. It is a gift to anyone seeking to live with greater awareness, kindness, and authenticity in today's fast-paced world.

Conclusion:

"The Things You Can See Only When You Slow Down" by Haemin Sunim is not just a book; it is a beacon of wisdom and tranquility in our hectic lives. It encourages us to pause, reflect, and embrace the beauty of the present moment—a timeless message that resonates deeply with readers of all backgrounds and walks of life.

Rating:

★★★★★
